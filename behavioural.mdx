# Behavioural Round — Prep

> ~15 minutes may go to behaviour / culture fit. NeoSigma is a tiny, intensely technical founding team (ex-MIT, Apple, Amazon, Essential AI). They're screening for **ownership, honesty, speed, and customer focus** — their four core values. Every story should quietly map to one of those values. Keep answers tight: 60–90 seconds each, STAR format, end on a result.

---

## 1. "Tell me about yourself" — the Hook (Value → Proof → Relevance)

Do **not** open with your title. Open with the value you create.

**Delivery (memorise the shape, not the words):**

> **(Value)** "I help teams ship products that feel genuinely high-quality — where the design and the engineering are the same craft, so nothing gets lost in handoff.
>
> **(Proof)** Over the last 3\+ years at MyBizz ERP I architected and published a component library that's now adopted across 6\+ products, cut initial page-load time by ~50% with code-splitting and lazy loading, and I lead both the frontend and the UI/UX team — so I own quality end to end, from the Figma file to production code.
>
> **(Relevance)** That's exactly why this role excites me: NeoSigma wants someone who designs _and_ builds the product experience without an engineer in between — and who holds the quality bar on how it looks, moves, and feels. That's the work I already do, and I want to do it on an AI-native product from the ground up."

**Why this lands:** curiosity in 10 seconds (value), one quantified proof, then a direct tie to _their_ JD language ("looks, moves, feels", "no engineer between your ideas and production").

---

## 2. "Why are you leaving / why do you want to leave your current job?"

Stay positive — never criticise your employer. Frame as moving _toward_ something.

> "I've grown a lot at MyBizz — I went from intern to SDE-II, built the design system, and now lead a team. What I'm looking for now is an environment with stronger alignment to how I like to work: smaller, intensely technical, high-ownership, where I can move fast and have direct impact on product direction. Reflecting on my long-term goals — being a design engineer who shapes both the craft and the technical foundation — this felt like the right time to seek that out. NeoSigma is exactly that kind of environment: founding team, real ownership, and a product where quality is the whole point."

**Key:** alignment with working style \+ growth \+ collaboration → not running _from_, running _toward_.

---

## 3. Core-value STAR stories — prepare ONE strong story per value

NeoSigma's four values. Have a crisp STAR story (Situation, Task, Action, Result) for each. Reuse your real experience — the prompts below map your résumé to each value.

### Customer Obsession — _start with the customer, work backwards_

**Story: Redesigning AutoDocSigner around the actual user workflow.**

- **S:** AutoDocSigner's signing flow was technically fine but users were getting lost.
- **T:** Improve the workflow, consistency, and overall experience — not just reskin it.
- **A:** Started from the user's job (sign a document securely with a USB token), mapped the real friction points, redesigned the flow and interface around that, and shipped it in code myself.
- **R:** Cleaner workflow, consistent interface, measurably smoother experience. _Tie-back: "I always start from the customer's job and work backwards to the UI — not the other way round."_

### Intellectual Honesty — _high trust, low ego, ideas over titles_

**Story: A design/architecture disagreement you handled openly.**

- **S:** Leading frontend \+ reviewing the UI/UX team's work, a junior pushed back on one of my component API decisions / a stakeholder wanted a pattern I thought was wrong.
- **T:** Reach the _right_ answer, not the answer that protects my title.
- **A:** Heard them out fully, assumed good intent, put both options on the table with trade-offs, and let the stronger argument win — even when it meant changing my own call.
- **R:** Better decision \+ a team that speaks up. _Tie-back: "I'd rather be corrected than be right by default — the idea matters more than who had it."_

### Bias for Action — _build and learn with customers, don't debate in the abstract_

**Story: The 50% page-load improvement, or shipping a high-conversion landing page fast.**

- **S:** Initial load was slow / a landing page was needed quickly for a launch.
- **T:** Ship a real improvement fast, not a perfect plan slowly.
- **A:** Profiled, applied code-splitting \+ lazy loading, shipped, measured, iterated on real metrics rather than theorising. (Or: prototyped the landing-page interactions directly instead of over-speccing.)
- **R:** ~50% faster initial load / a high-conversion page live on time. _Tie-back: "I prefer building and measuring over debating — real feedback beats a whiteboard."_

### Extreme Ownership — _outcomes, not tasks; see it through to working in practice_

**Story: Building MyBizz ERP from scratch as an intern → owning it to production.**

- **S:** Joined as a student intern with the product needing to be built from Figma to production.
- **T:** Not "do my tickets" — make the product actually work for users.
- **A:** Owned frontend architecture, the reusable component system, and core modules (auth, roles, invoicing, accounting), partnered directly with the founder on vision, aligned API contracts with backend — and stayed with it through to a shipped, adopted product over 3\+ years.
- **R:** A platform now live across multiple products; I grew into leading it. _Tie-back: "I take responsibility for the outcome working in practice, not just for closing my tasks."_

> **Prep tip:** write the _first sentence_ of each story so you can launch cleanly under pressure. The rest flows once you start.

## 4. Other likely behavioural questions

**"Tell me about a time you disagreed with a teammate/manager."** → use the Intellectual Honesty story.

**"A project you're most proud of?"** → the component library / UI Kit: "I built the layer 6\+ products are built on. Proud because it's _leverage_ — one well-designed primitive raises quality everywhere at once."

**"How do you handle feedback / criticism on your designs?"** → "I separate ego from the work. Critique is about the artifact, not me. I lead design reviews, so I model receiving feedback well — I ask _why_ before defending, and I change my mind when the reason is good."

**"A time you failed / a mistake?"** → pick something real, own it cleanly, end on the lesson and the systemic fix you put in place (e.g., a regression that led you to add reviews/tests/process). Ownership \> excuses.

**"How do you mentor / lead?"** → "Through code reviews, pair programming, and design direction — I mentored 4 juniors and lifted team velocity ~20%. I lead by raising the standard and making it reachable, not by gatekeeping."

**"Why NeoSigma / why this role specifically?"** → "Three things: it's a _design-engineer_ role where I own look-and-feel _and_ the code — my exact sweet spot. It's a founding-team scope with real ownership and a path to technical leadership. And it's AI-native — making non-deterministic systems observable and trustworthy is a genuinely new design problem, and I want to work on the new thing, not the settled thing."

**"Where do you see yourself / what do you want to grow into?"** → "A technical leader who still ships — owning the component library, the quality bar, and eventually the design-engineering culture as the team scales." (The JD explicitly offers this path — mirror it.)

**"What are you looking for in your next role?"** → ownership, a high quality bar, a small fast team, and direct product impact. (All true of NeoSigma — say so.)

## 5. Questions to ask them (always have 3–4 ready)

Asking sharp questions is itself a signal of seniority and genuine interest.

- "As the founding design engineer, what does the component library look like today — am I starting it, or evolving something? Where's the quality bar right now versus where you want it?"
- "How do you think about the design–engineering loop on the team today? Where does quality currently leak?"
- "What does 'AI-native interface' mean to _you_ for NeoSigma's product specifically — what's the hardest unsolved UX problem in making self-improving agents observable?"
- "In the first 90 days, what would make you say this hire was clearly the right call?"
- "How does a small, intensely technical team make design decisions — what's the process when taste disagrees?"

---

## 6. Tone reminders

- **Concise \+ confident.** STAR, quantify, land on a result. Don't ramble — a tight 75-second story beats a three-minute one.
- **Map every story to a value** without naming it robotically — let the behaviour show it.
- **Positive about the past, excited about the future.** No bitterness about the current job.
- **Match their register:** high-trust, low-ego, direct. Strong opinions, openly held, good faith. That _is_ Intellectual Honesty — demonstrate it in how you talk, not just in a story.
- **You belong in the room:** 3\+ years, intern→SDE-II, a published design system across 6\+ products, and you lead both eng and design. Carry that quiet confidence.
