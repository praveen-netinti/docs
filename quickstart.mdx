title: "Quickstart"
description: "Begin with a guide on the fastest path to a successful outcome"
---

Here's a set of natural reactions, grouped by the moment you'd use them. Mix them up — using the same one every time is worse than using none.

Acknowledging a good question (then go straight into the answer)

- "That's a good question."
- "Interesting question."
- "Good one — I actually thought about this a fair bit."
- "Yeah, this is a fun one."
- "That's something I had to make a real decision on."
- "Good question, and it's one I'd push back on a little."

Buying a couple of seconds to think (use sparingly, sound calm not stalling)

- "Let me think for a second."
- "Give me a moment to think that through."
- "Let me think about how to put this."
- "Hmm, let me actually think about that properly."
- "That's worth thinking through for a second."

Agreeing / validating their point

- "Yeah, exactly."
- "Right, that's a fair point."
- "You're right, that's a real gap."
- "Totally agree, that one bugged me too."
- "Yeah, I noticed that as well."
- "Good catch — that's something I'd fix."

Transitioning into your answer

- "So my thinking there was..."
- "The main reason was..."
- "The way I approached it was..."
- "So here's how I looked at it..."
- "In this case, what I did was..."

When you partly disagree (stay friendly, not defensive)

- "I see it a little differently, actually."
- "I'd push back on that gently — here's why."
- "That's true, though there's a trade-off I'd point out."
- "Fair, but in this case I went the other way because..."

When you don't know something (never fake it)

- "I haven't worked with that directly, but here's how I'd approach it."
- "I'm not completely sure, so rather than guess, I'd start by..."
- "I haven't had the chance to use that in production, but from what I understand..."
- "Honestly, I'd have to look into that — my first instinct would be..."

When adding to or continuing an answer

- "The other thing worth mentioning is..."
- "And if I had more time..."
- "One more thing on that..."
- "To build on that..."

Light reactions to keep it conversational

- "Sounds great."
- "Yeah, makes sense."
- "Right, fair enough."
- "Good — happy to dig into that."

A couple of delivery notes: keep the opener to two or three words and then move — don't let "that's a really interesting question" become a habit, because interviewers notice it as a stall. And for the "let me think" ones, it's fine to actually pause silently for a second or two; a calm pause reads as more senior than filling the air.
